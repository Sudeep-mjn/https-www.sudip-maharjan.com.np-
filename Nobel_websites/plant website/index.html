Plant Care Essentials

Learn how to keep your plants thriving with our expert care tips.

Light Requirements

Understanding your plant's light needs is crucial for its health. Most indoor plants thrive in bright, indirect light.

South-facing windows provide the most light, while north-facing offer the least. Rotate your plants regularly for even growth. Signs of too much light include scorched leaves, while leggy growth indicates insufficient light.

Watering Wisdom

Overwatering is the most common cause of plant death. Learn to water based on your plant's needs, not a schedule.

Check soil moisture by sticking your finger 1-2 inches into the soil. Most plants prefer to dry out slightly between waterings. Use room-temperature water and ensure proper drainage. Succulents need less frequent watering than tropical plants.

Humidity & Temperature

Many houseplants originate from tropical environments and appreciate higher humidity levels.

Group plants together to create a humid microclimate, use a pebble tray, or consider a humidifier. Most houseplants prefer temperatures between 65-75°F (18-24°C). Avoid placing plants near drafts or heating/cooling vents.
